Three Randomization Dimensions
Each dimension tests a different fragility hypothesis · independent · all 3 applied per simulation
DIMENSION 1 · TRADE ORDER
Random Trade Order
trades_shuffled = shuffle(trades)
WHAT
Randomly permute the chronological order of trades from the backtest ledger. Same trades, different sequence.
DISTRIBUTION
Uniform random permutation (Fisher-Yates shuffle) · seeded for reproducibility
WHY
Tests sensitivity to trade sequencing. Live trade order is not deterministic — strategy may experience clustered losses early. Tests drawdown sequence risk.
DETECTS
Strategies that survive in chronological order but blow up when losses cluster. Drawdown sequence fragility.
METRICS AFFECTED
Max Drawdown (sequence-sensitive), Sharpe (slightly), Recovery Factor, Risk of Ruin
DIMENSION 2 · SLIPPAGE
Random Slippage
slip_i ~ LogNormal(μ, σ) per trade
WHAT
For each trade, sample a slippage value from a log-normal distribution calibrated to the broker's live P50/P90/P99.
DISTRIBUTION
LogNormal(μ, σ) · μ = ln(P50), σ derived from (P90-P50) and (P99-P50) · broker-specific
WHY
Tests sensitivity to execution cost variance. Live slippage is stochastic — a strategy that works at P50 slippage may fail at P99 on every trade.
DETECTS
Strategies whose edge is consumed by tail slippage events. Execution cost fragility.
METRICS AFFECTED
Cost Drag, Profit Factor, CAGR (post-cost), Sharpe (post-cost)
DIMENSION 3 · SPREAD
Random Spread
spread_i ~ Beta(α, β) × max_spread
WHAT
For each trade, sample a spread multiplier (1× to 5× baseline) from a Beta distribution. Models news widening + session variation.
DISTRIBUTION
Beta(α=2, β=5) × max_spread (5× baseline) · calibrated to broker's spread baseline + stdev
WHY
Tests sensitivity to spread variance. Live spreads spike unpredictably (news, liquidity gaps). Strategy may look profitable at average spread but lose at widened spread.
DETECTS
Strategies that depend on tight spreads. Spread widening fragility.
METRICS AFFECTED
Cost Drag, Profit Factor, Win Rate (cost-sensitive exits), Sharpe
WHY THESE 3 DIMENSIONS?
Each dimension tests a different fragility that backtests hide
Backtests use chronological order, fixed P50 slippage, and fixed baseline spread — all of which are convenient fictions. Live trading has random trade order (clustering), stochastic slippage (P99 tail), and widening spreads (news events). The 3 dimensions independently test each fragility: if a strategy survives 10,000 sims across all 3, it is robust to the realities of live execution. Survival Score < 95% means the strategy is fragile to one of these 3 dimensions and will likely fail in live trading.
